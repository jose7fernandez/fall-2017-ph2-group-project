Thunder Hole, Acadia National Park

Breathtaking in its natural beauty and covering an area of 49,052 acres, Maine's Acadia National Park features mountains with granite peaks, an ocean shoreline, woodlands and lakes. The tallest mountain on the Atlantic coast of the United States, Cadillac Mountain, stands inside Acadia National Park. Thunder Hole is one of the most popular stops inside the park to enjoy its natural beauty.

Bubble Pond, Acadia National Park

Bubble Pond is a beautiful pond nestled between mountains which is about 40 feet deep. It is a wonderful spot for sitting or viewing along the shoreline. The carriage road and stone bridge nearby are perfect for a bike ride or walk.

Carriage Road, Acadia National Park

John D. Rockefeller Jr. gifted 45 miles of carriage roads which weave around the mountains and valleys of Acadia National Park. Rockefeller was a skilled horseman who wanted to travel by horse and carriage on motor-free byways. His construction efforts from 1913 to 1940 resulted in this road offering sweeping vistas of the landscape.
