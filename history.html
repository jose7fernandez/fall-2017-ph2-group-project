History


Mount Desert Island remained a sparsely populated collection of fishing villages until Thomas Cole arrived on the island in 1844. His paintings revealed the beauty of the island to the outside world. By 1880 many hotels had sprung up to keep pace with the ever growing number of vacationers. Tourism was becoming the main industry on the island. In 1919 President Woodrow Wilson set aside 5,000 acres of the island to create the Sieur de Monts National Monument. It became a national park three years later and was eventually named Acadia, the French word for "heaven on earth"
